What is Euler’s Method?

  • A simple, numerical procedure for solving differential equations.

  • Approximates solutions by taking small, finite steps along the path of the solution.

  • Relies on the concept of the slope or rate of change at a given point.

  • Therefore requires the function to be differentiable.

Euler’s Method: The Basics

  • Start at a known point on the curve.

  • Determine the slope (rate of change) at this point.

  • Take a small step along this slope to the next point.

  • Repeat the process.

Euler’s method: The steps

  1. Define a differential equation: \(\frac{d}{dx} f(x) = f(x)\)
  2. Define the initial condition: \(x_0 = 0, f(x_0) = 1\)
  3. Define the step size: \(\Delta x = 1\)
  4. Plot initial condition
  5. \(f(x_1) = f(x_0) + \frac{d}{dx} f(x_0) \times \Delta x\)

Next value of \(f(x)\) is estimated as the previous value plus the amount of change in \(f(x)\) since the last step.

Euler’s method doesn’t fit very well!?!?

  • Euler’s Method is very sensitive to the step size
  • The smaller the step size, the better the fit
  • Larger step sizes can be okay if the rate of change is small

Smaller the step size \(\rightarrow\) better fit

  • \(\frac{d}{dx}f(x) \Delta x = 0.1\)

Larger step size okay if \(\frac{d}{dx}f(x)\) is small

  • \(\Delta x = 1, \frac{d}{dx} f(x) = \frac{1}{100} f(x)\)

Choosing a step size

  • The smaller the step, the closer the approximation to the true curve.

  • However, smaller steps increase computational effort.

  • Small steps may not be needed if function changes slowly.

  • Finding a balance is key.
