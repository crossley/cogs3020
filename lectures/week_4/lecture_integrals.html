The Concept of an Integral

  • The integral of a function represents the accumulated area under the curve of the function with respect to one of its input variables.

  • It helps us understand how quantities accumulate over time or space, providing a total sum from continuous data.

  • The integral of a function \(f(x)\) over an interval \([a, b]\) is denoted as \(\int_a^b f(x) \,dx\).

Example: \(f(x) = x\)

Example: \(f(x) = x^2\)

Example: \(f(x) = \sin(x)\)
