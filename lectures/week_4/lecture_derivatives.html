The Concept of a Derivative

  • The derivative of a function is the rate of change of the function with respect to one of its input variables.

  • It tells us how fast or slow a quantity (represented by the function) is changing at any point.

  • The derivative of a function \(f(x)\) at a point \(x\) is denoted as \(f'(x)\) or \(\frac{df}{dx}\).

Example: \(f(x) = x^2\)

Example: \(f(x) = x^3\)

Example: \(f(x) = x^4\)
